Analysis on Break and Enters in Toronto - 2020

Link here

This was a group statistics project done for my 1st year statistics course: STA130. The purpose of this project is to analyze the break and enter data given to us by the Analytics and Innovation Team at the Toronto Police Service to make recommendations to reduce/combat the amount of break and enters in Toronto.

The project demonstrates the ability to use R to produce visualizations and conduct hypothesis tests to answer our research questions.

A fun fact is that this assignment was one of the few assignments chosen by our professor to send to the Toronto Police Services.

Analysis of Toronto's Homeless Population - 2021

Link here

This is a statistics project for my 2nd year statistics course: STA238. The purpose of this project is to analyze some data using bootstrap confidence intervals.

This project comes in two parts: Part 1 shows an understanding of Bayesian statistics, Part 2 is an analysis on the 2020 homeless population in Toronto.

This project demonstrates an understanding of R, Bayesian statistics, LaTeX and Empirical Bootstrapping methods.
